About Our Technology

Revolutionizing bone health monitoring through acoustic technology

The Problem

Traditional bone densitometers are bulky and impractical for space missions where every kilogram matters. Additionally, aging populations need accessible, affordable bone health monitoring to prevent osteoporosis-related fractures.

Current bone health assessment methods rely on expensive, stationary equipment like DEXA scanners that are unavailable in remote locations, space missions, or for routine home monitoring.

Our Solution

We've developed a lightweight, wearable device that uses acoustic technology to assess bone health. Our system employs:

  • Vibration Motors: Generate controlled vibrations (DC 3V, 12000rpm) in bone tissue to stimulate acoustic responses
  • Piezoelectric Film Sensors: Detect acoustic responses with high sensitivity, measuring damping factors and impedance
  • ESP32 S3 DevKitC: Process data in real-time and provide wireless connectivity via Bluetooth/Wi-Fi
  • LCD Display: Show real-time feedback to users with intuitive health status indicators

How It Works

1

Vibration Generation

The device emits low-frequency acoustic waves (0.5-2 MHz) through the skin to the bone. Vibration motors create controlled mechanical oscillations that propagate through tissue.

2

Signal Detection

Piezoelectric sensors capture the acoustic response and measure damping factors. The sensor detects how the bone absorbs and reflects the acoustic energy.

3

Data Analysis

The ESP32 computes acoustic impedance and compares it to healthy baseline ranges. Advanced algorithms filter out tissue interference and isolate bone-specific signals.

4

Health Assessment

Results are displayed instantly on the LCD and synchronized to the web dashboard, alerting users to any concerning changes in bone density or quality.

Scientific Foundation

Our approach is based on peer-reviewed research showing that bone strength correlates with mineral content, modulus of elasticity, and natural frequency of bone vibration. Studies have demonstrated that acoustic sweep monitoring can be equal to or better than mineral density measurements as a predictor of bone strength.

The method measures material damping using excitation in the acoustic range, providing insights into bone quality beyond simple density measurements. This includes:

  • Assessment of trabecular bone microarchitecture
  • Detection of early-stage osteoporotic changes
  • Monitoring of bone adaptation in microgravity environments
  • Non-invasive evaluation of bone mechanical competence
