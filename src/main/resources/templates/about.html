About Our Technology

Revolutionizing bone health monitoring through acoustic technology

The Problem

Traditional bone densitometers are bulky and impractical for space missions where every kilogram matters. Additionally, aging populations need accessible, affordable bone health monitoring to prevent osteoporosis-related fractures.

Our Solution

We've developed a lightweight, wearable device that uses acoustic technology to assess bone health. Our system employs:

  • Vibration Motors: Generate controlled vibrations in bone tissue
  • Piezoelectric Film Sensors: Detect acoustic responses with high sensitivity
  • ESP32 S3 DevKitC: Process data and provide wireless connectivity
  • LCD Display: Show real-time feedback to users

How It Works

1

Vibration Generation

The device emits low-frequency acoustic waves (0.5-2 MHz) through the skin to the bone.

2

Signal Detection

Piezoelectric sensors capture the acoustic response and measure damping factors.

3

Data Analysis

Arduino computes acoustic impedance and compares it to healthy baseline ranges.

4

Health Assessment

Results are displayed instantly, alerting users to any concerning changes.

Scientific Foundation

Our approach is based on research showing that bone strength correlates with mineral content, modulus of elasticity, and natural frequency of bone vibration. Studies have demonstrated that acoustic sweep monitoring can be equal to or better than mineral density measurements as a predictor of bone strength.

The method measures material damping using excitation in the acoustic range, providing insights into bone quality beyond simple density measurements.
